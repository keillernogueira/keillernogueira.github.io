Welcome!

I am a Lecturer and part of the Machine Learning Research Group within the School of Computer Science and Informatics at the University of Liverpool, UK. I received my PhD in Computer Science from the Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais (UFMG), Brazil, with part of my doctoral research conducted at GIPSA-Lab, Institut Polytechnique de Grenoble, France.

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, deep learning, pattern recognition, image processing, and remote sensing.

Research

My research develops machine and deep learning methods for image understanding, with a strong emphasis on real-world Earth observation applications where labelled data is scarce.

🛰️
Remote Sensing & Earth Observation
I develop deep learning methods for large-scale semantic segmentation, scene classification, land cover mapping, and change detection in satellite and aerial imagery. A key focus is building models that generalise across geographic regions and sensor types, reducing the annotation burden for operational Earth observation pipelines.
Pattern Recognition'17 IEEE GRSL'18 IEEE Access'21 IJRS'25 arXiv'25
🏷️
Weakly Supervised & Data-Efficient Learning
Pixel-level annotations for remote sensing are expensive and scarce. My work explores weakly supervised approaches — using image-level, point, or low-resolution labels — and data-efficient techniques such as core-set selection and label refinement to train high-performing segmentation models with less supervision.
IJRS'25 arXiv'25 Core-Set Machine Learning'23
🔷
Deep Morphological Networks
Standard convolutional networks are built on linear operations. I investigate replacing convolutions with non-linear morphological operations (erosion, dilation, opening, closing) to build Deep Morphological Networks that can naturally capture structural patterns in images that linear filters miss.
IEEE Access'21 arXiv'19
🌿
Precision Agriculture & Vegetation Analysis
I apply deep learning to agricultural monitoring tasks including coffee crop recognition, citrus orchard segmentation, tree canopy detection in urban environments, and vegetation species discrimination using multispectral UAV and satellite imagery.
CIARP'15 PRRS'16 SIBGRAPI'17 IGARSS'21
🔓
Open Set Recognition & Segmentation
Real-world imagery contains classes unseen during training. I develop open set semantic segmentation methods — including OpenFCN and OpenPCS — that leverage feature-space analysis to identify and reject unknown classes rather than forcing predictions into a fixed closed set.
Machine Learning'23
📊
Incremental & Small-Sample Learning
In collaboration with colleagues at the University of Stirling, I work on probabilistic incremental learning methods (GMM-IL, NS-IL) designed for image classification and visual question answering under small sample regimes, where standard deep networks overfit or fail entirely.
IEEE Access'23 (GMM-IL) IEEE Access'23 (NS-IL)
